# Affordability × Child Safety - Slide Deck

## Overview

This Next.js application reuses the same 16:9 slide shell and navigation pattern as the attached Story4.2 deck, but it reframes food insecurity as an affordability and child-safety issue that Democrats can use in Republican-leaning states.

## Slide progression

1. **Thesis** - Food insecurity is a pocketbook issue Democrats can win in red states.
2. **States** - The highest-scarcity states are already Republican-leaning.
3. **Affordability** - Food insecurity and Republican margins move together.
4. **Children** - Child hunger, school meals, and the safety frame.
5. **Targets** - Child poverty remains elevated as children age into adulthood.
6. **Sources** - Data inputs, method, limits, and political takeaway.

## Data files

```text
public/data/story6-state-data.json
public/data/story6-summary.json
```

The joined dataset uses USDA ERS 2022-2024 average state food insecurity rates, Census poverty-by-age data, and 2024 presidential results.

## Key finding

Food insecurity is strongly associated with child poverty and remains politically salient in Republican-leaning states. The winning message is affordability plus child safety, not a blunt anti-hunger pitch.

## Presentation style

- 16:9 fixed slide frame
- One message per slide
- Consistent header/footer/navigation
- Simple charts, sparse annotation, and a political messaging frame
